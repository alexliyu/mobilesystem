Library Search

Search Oxford University's extensive library collections on the move. If you have a phone that supports geo-location, Library search will show you a map of the libraries with availability of your book*.

Tip: The 'Universal search' box on the front page will automatically search for a book if you enter its ISBN.

*Be aware that you may not have access to all libraries (especially those contained in colleges).

1. Search for a book

2. Find the right edition

WebLearn Mobile Preview

With this "preview" version of WebLearn mobile you can book 'sign-up' events that have been set up by your WebLearn site manager e.g. Tutorials by a course tutor.

We use a technology called "oAuth" to let you access very limited parts of WebLearn through {{ site_name }} without having to re-enter your password every time. To do this, follow the instructions on screen when you first click on the "Sign-Ups" tool (as seen in these screenshots). If at anytime you want to stop your phone having access to your WebLearn account you may stop it by using the instructions on {{ site_name }} or in WebLearn follow the "My workspace"->"Trusted applications" to remove {{ site_name }}.

1.The first page in the one-time authorisation process

2. Select your appropriate account type. NB: Whenever logging into any service, make sure the URL (the address) is one you recognise.

3. WebLearn then asks you to confirm that you want to be able to access WebLearn through {{ site_name }}.

4. Then you can book events!

Search

You will find effective search boxes on all the key pages across {{ site_name }}, but they are a little smarter than you might think!

Front page 'Universal Search'

You will find this search box which searches all of {{ site_name }} on the front page.

'App specific' searches

On each app (e.g. "Podcasts" or "Places") you will find another search box. These search boxes will only search the contents of that specific app i.e. if you search for "History of Art" inside the "Podcasts" app, it will only show you podcasts related to the search.

The 'Universal search' Box

The search box found in the 'News' app

Special Searches
Bus Stop Numbers
Typing in a bus stop number will take you straight to that stop's real time information page
Oxontime numbers (as on all bus stops in Oxfordshire) e.g. "69328969", a city centre bus stop number e.g. "E3", or an ATCO code e.g. "69328969".
ISBN/ISSN Numbers
Type in an ISBN/ISSN number and {{ site_name }} will run a Library Search for you.
Postcodes
Type in a post code and you can use it as a starting point for finding things near by in the "Places" app.

News

Quick and mobile optimised access to feeds from around the University are available here. Images are resized to fit your device and are designed to be quick to load.

Tip: you can search all our news items by using the search box at the top of the news page or the universal search on the front page.

Don't see your favourite University news source? Drop us a line: mobileoxford@oucs.ox.ac.uk and we'll do our best to put it up.

Podcasts

Full access to the University of Oxford's public podcasts library as found on http://podcasts.ox.ac.uk/ and iTunes U, but designed to be easy to browse on your device.

iPhone Tip: When you click on 'podcasts' you will be asked whether you'd like to use your built in iTunes App to browse our podcasts. This gives you a few advantages like being able to queue downloads and store them in your library.

Data Sources

ACIS - Oxontime
Oxfordshire's real time bus information in conjunction with Oxford Bus Company, Stagecoach, Thames Travel and Arriva
Department for Transport
The UK's NaPTAN database allowing us to map the locations of bus stops, railway stations and other transport access nodes.
Oxfordshire County Council
Information about the city's Park and Rides and access to the real time transport information provided by ACIS.
Oxford University Exam Schools
Letting us know when exam results have been released.
OUCS Podcasting Team
Access to the University of Oxford's podcasts as mirrored on their iTunesU site.
Oxford University Library Services
Access to their Z39.50 library information system allowing us to perform library searches and combine with our geo-spatial systems to provide location-sensitive searches
The Open Street Map Community
Providing some of the most detailed and up to date maps of Oxford and points of interest listings.
BBC Backstage
Weather and traffic incident information
